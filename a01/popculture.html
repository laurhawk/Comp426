Vampires in Popular Culture

Vampires turned into our modern perception after the publication of The Vampyre in 1819 and Dracula in 1897. Below are some examples of vampires in popular culture.

The modern vampire is an aristocratic, sexual, and charming figure. It is supposed to be liked despite its tendency to kill.

  • Lord Ruthven (Based on Byron)
  • Count Dracula
  • Lestat from Anne Rice's The Vampire Chronicles
  • Edward Cullen from Twilight
  • Grandpa Munster from The Munsters
  • Elena Gilbert from The Vampire Diaries
  • The Count from Sesame Street
  • Angel and Spike from Buffy the Vampire Slayer
  • The clan from The Lost Boys
  • Blade from Marvel's Blade comics
  • Nosferatu from Nosferatu (and Spongebob)
